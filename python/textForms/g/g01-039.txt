None of these classes, except the yeomen, paid or expected to pay direct taxes. Dur- ing the fourteenth century, the traditional methods of financing the Exchequer had become stabilized. When the King and his Council required additional funds, they were usually granted an export tax on the wool trade, collected by means of that 'sta- ple' system which ensured that prices, quality and tax could be efficiently supervised and controlled, together with a subsidy or tax on all movable property.
